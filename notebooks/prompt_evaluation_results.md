- Ensure ready access to emergency evacuation routes and have a go-bag prepared.

#### Strategy B: Detailed  *(~218 words)*

**1. RISK ASSESSMENT**

The model assigns a 99.3% fire probability — effectively certain under current
conditions. The three biggest drivers are the **Initial Spread Index (ISI 16.0)**,
**Fine Fuel Moisture Code (FFMC 92.0)**, and **Fire Weather Index (FWI 29.0)**.
Together they signal that fine surface fuels are bone-dry, ignition is extremely
easy, and any fire that starts will spread rapidly.

**2. WHAT THE INDICES MEAN**

- **FFMC (92.0):** Measures moisture in the fine surface litter (leaves, grass,
  twigs). At 92, these fuels are critically dry and will ignite from a single spark.
- **ISI (16.0):** Estimates how fast a fire will spread based on wind and FFMC.
  A value above 13 is considered extreme.
- **FWI (29.0):** The overall fire danger rating. Values above 20 are classified
  as Very High to Extreme across most fire danger systems.

**3. ACTION PLAN**

1. Avoid all outdoor burning, barbecues, or spark-producing activities.
2. Prepare and review your household evacuation plan.
3. Pack a 72-hour emergency go-bag (documents, medication, water, phone charger).
4. Stay tuned to local fire authority alerts — be ready to leave immediately.
5. If you see smoke, call emergency services without delay.

**4. LIMITATIONS**

This model was trained on Algerian summer data (2012) and does not account for
real-time local topography, vegetation type, or suppression resources.

## Analysis & Conclusion

**Strategy A (Brief)** produces concise, readable outputs but lacks explanation
of *why* the risk is elevated and does not explain FWI indices. Suitable as a
quick-glance summary but insufficient as a standalone response for users who
want to understand the underlying reasoning.

**Strategy B (Detailed)** is the most complete. It explicitly links the
ML model's top features to the risk explanation, provides a plain-language
glossary of key FWI indices, and gives a prioritised numbered action plan.
Slightly longer (~200 words), but well within a comfortable reading length.
**Selected as the default strategy.**

**Strategy C (Structured)** uses markdown headings, making it easy to parse
programmatically or render in separate UI cards. However, the constrained
format sometimes forces generic phrasing. Used as an alternative rendering
mode for the structured output panel in the Gradio app.

**Integration with ML block:** Every strategy receives the ML prediction
label, probability, and top-3 feature importances as explicit context.
The LLM is instructed to reference these values directly, ensuring the
NLP output is grounded in — and explains — the numeric model's decision.
This tight coupling is the core integration point between the two blocks.
